Home / Welcome to Ohio

We love to have visitors at the Hudson office, and we want to make sure that your visit is a great one!  Here are a few tips that we have put together to help plan your trip to Ohio, and please don’t hesitate to contact us if you have any questions or need additional information.  We’ll see you soon!

Where is the Hudson Office? 

The office address is 1780 Stoney Hill Drive - Suite A, Hudson OH  44236.  Hudson is located in Northeast Ohio, between Akron and Cleveland.

How Do I Get There from Here?

Both Cleveland Hopkins Airport (CLE - http://www.clevelandairport.com) and Akron Canton Airport (CAK - http://www.akroncantonairport.com/) are 45-60 minutes away from the office.  Cleveland tends to have more flights, but Akron Canton is smaller and easier to navigate and often has lower fares.

We recommend renting a car for your visit – the office is some distance from the airports and public transportation is not available.  There is limited taxi service, and both airport sites have listings for taxi companies.  However, a car tends to be more flexible and much more economical.  There are a number of rental car options at each airport.

Driving from the Cleveland Airport, take OH-237 North to I-480 East.  You will stay on I-480 E for 26 miles, to Exit 37 OH-91/Darrow Road.  Take Darrow Road south for 5 miles.  The office is at the corner of Darrow Road and Stoney Hill Drive, approximately 1 mile south of downtown Hudson.

Driving from the Akron Canton Airport, take I-77 North for 11 miles to OH-8 North.  Continue on OH-8 N for 9 miles to the Steels Corners exit.  Turn Right at Steels Corners and continue onto Hudson Drive for 3 miles.  Turn Right at Terex Road, and then take the first left onto Darrow Road.  The office is at the corner of Darrow Road and Stoney Hill Drive, approximately 1 mile north of Terex Road.

 

Where Should I Stay?

There are a number of hotel options near the office, depending on your personal preferences.

One of the nicest hotels in the area is the Sheraton Suites in Cuyahoga Falls (http://www.sheratonakron.com/).  It is located next to the Cuyahoga River - be sure to request a riverside room!  There is a restaurant on site, as well as some shops and other great dining options within walking distance.  The hotel is located about 15 minutes south of the office, with easy access to OH-8.

There are no full service hotels in the Hudson downtown area, but there is a bed and breakfast for those who prefer that option.  The Jeremiah B. King Guest House (http://www.kingguesthouse.com/) allows you to stay in a historic Hudson home, within easy walking distance of the downtown shops and restaurants.  It has amenities for business travelers, and often hosts visitors of the Western Reserve Academy, one of the top boarding schools in the country.  It is about a mile from the office.

If you prefer a well-known hotel chain closer to the office, there are two hotels located at the Steels Corners exit of OH-8.  The Hampton Inn (http://hamptoninn.hilton.com) is located at 4331 Lakepointe Corporate Drive in Stow.  There is also a Courtyard by Marriott (http://www.marriott.com/hotels/travel/cakcy-courtyard-akron-stow/).  Both are about 10 minutes from the office.

Other hotels may be found in Streetsboro, which is also about a 10 minute drive from the office.  These include a Fairfield Inn, Holiday Inn Express, and Wingate by Wyndham.

What is the Weather Like in Hudson?

Around here, we say if you don’t like the weather, just wait a few minutes, because it will change! 

The weather in Ohio tends to be fairly moderate.  The high temperatures in the summer are typically between 80 and 90 degrees F (27 – 32C).  Winter temperatures may reach below zero (-18C) with wind chill, and snowfalls of 12 inches (30 cm) or more in the winter months are not uncommon.  Spring and fall tend to be highly variable, and conditions can change rapidly from day to day.  We recommend that you check the local forecast before visiting if weather is a concern. 

Please be advised that, particularly in the winter months, the weather is likely to impact travel conditions.  If you are uncomfortable driving in snowy and icy conditions, you may want to take advantage of taxi services rather than renting a car if severe weather is forecast.

 

What is There to Do in Hudson?

There are an amazing number of things to do in the area around Hudson, from outdoor activities to world-renowned attractions that bring tourists from around the world.

The tremendous variation in seasons allows us to enjoy a wide variety of outdoor activities.  In the winter, you can enjoy skiing, sledding, and tubing.  In the summer, there are a number of swimming areas, as well as opportunities to go boating, fishing, and canoeing.  Year-round, we enjoy hiking and biking; there are a large number of public parks that are spectacular in the spring and fall.

If you are interested in sports, Cleveland has professional football, baseball, and basketball teams.  There are also minor league hockey, baseball, and soccer teams.  In Canton, you might visit the Pro Football Hall of Fame (http://www.profootballhof.com/default.aspx).

For those who enjoy music, the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (http://rockhall.com/) is located in Cleveland.  There are also a number of other performance venues, including the Blossom Music Center (http://www.theblossommusiccenter.com/) and Playhouse Square (http://www.playhousesquare.org/). 

Here are a few more of our favorite places to visit:

  • Cuyahoga Valley National Park (http://www.nps.gov/cuva/index.htm) – about 10 minutes from the office, with extensive hiking and biking opportunities.  Stroll past historic locks from the days of the Ohio and Erie Canal, see amazing wildlife, including blue herons, deer, and even bald eagles, and bike along the Towpath Trail.  There is also a train that runs through the park, which you can ride with your bike.
  • Century Cycles (http://centurycycles.com/about/peninsula-pg288.htm) – located in Peninsula, about 10 minutes from the office and next to the Towpath Trail.  Rent bikes and helmets to enjoy a day in the national park.
  • Snow Tubing at Brandywine (http://www.bmbw.com/snow-tubing-polar-blast.html) – about 25 minutes from the office.  Spend a day plummeting down the chutes in polar innertubes.
  • Skiing at Boston Mills (http://www.bmbw.com/) – about 25 minutes from the office.  Skiing and snow tubing, with a lodge and rental equipment available.
  • Cedar Point (http://www.cedarpoint.com/) – about 90 minutes from the office.  A world-class amusement park, with some of the biggest roller coasters on the planet.
  • First and Main Shopping Area in Hudson (http://www.firstandmainhudson.com/) – 1 mile north of the office.  A variety of shopping and dining options, all pedestrian friendly.  There are a number of notable shops, including an independent bookstore  complete with a book store dog, two toy shops with unusual and high-quality toys for children, and a number of other small retailers offering personalized service.
  • Crooked River Adventures (http://www.kent.edu/crookedriver/index.cfm) – located in Kent, about 15 minutes from the office.  Rent a canoe or kayak to travel down the Cuyahoga River.
 

We look forward to welcoming you to the Hudson office!  If you have any other questions, or if there is anything that we can do to make your stay more enjoyable, please let us know!
